Mozilla Firefox Origin

Mozilla Firefox is a free and open-source webbrowser which was developed by the Mozilla-Project. Nowadays Mozilla is not used as widely as before, but the browser is still very popular with webdevelopers because it follows W3C web standards better than most other browsers. Also ther are a lot of very usefull extension avalible for Firefox. In January 2015 there were a total of 18.000 ad-ons.

Mozilla Firefox Tips and Tricks

More Screen Space

Firefox allows you to reduce the size of the icons and your toolbar. It is only a small change, but it can save some much needed screenspace on a tablet, or just give a more minmalistic look.

Quick Bookmarking

With one click you can bookmark any webpage that you are currently on. By simply clicking the star icon on the right of the toolbar, Firefox save a bookmark of the specific website your are on, so you can acces it easily any time you want.

Personalized Toolbar

In firefox you can personlize your toolbar and add any icons you need. This allows you to print, download share or do anything you need on a webpage with just one click.

Set Up Multiple Homepages

Firefox also alows you to set up multiple homepages. So if you can't chose which websites need to open when you first launch your browser, Firefox can just open all of them for you at once.

Password Vault Lockwise

Firefox can store all your passwords safely and secure in their vault called Lockwise, so that you don't have to remeber every password on every website, or have the same password for many websites, which of course, is never a good idea. All you have to do, is think of one master password, and this allows you to acces your vault. Firefox will automaticly fill in any details that are saved for that specific website.

Increase the speed

If you feel Firefox is running a bit slow, you can give it some extra power by turning on hardware acceleration. Just go to About: Preferences > General > Performance and uncheck the box that says: Use Recommended Performance Settings. A new checkbox should appear that says Use hardware acceleration when available, just check the box, and it should give your browser some extra power if avalible.

Screen Grab

This feature currently is integrated in most opertaing systems already but it's still a nice feature to have. Firefox allows you to very easily take screenshot or partial screenshots of a webpage and allows you to store them instantly. You can make a screenshot by right clicking anywher on a page, and clicking the Page Actions menu. Now just select the area you need a screenshot from, and you're done!
